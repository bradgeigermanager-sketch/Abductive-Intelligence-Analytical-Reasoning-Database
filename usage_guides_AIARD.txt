Documentation at **three levels simultaneously**:

1. **Human Analyst Guide** (how to use it)
2. **Machine-Readable Data Dictionary** (what every field means)
3. **Reasoning Ontology Specification** (how concepts relate)

This is critical because the database is not merely storing data—it is storing **reasoning, uncertainty, evidence, and knowledge**.

# Abductive Intelligence Analytical Reasoning Database (AIARD)

## System Purpose

AIARD is designed to capture:

```text
Reality
↓
Observation
↓
Evidence
↓
Hypothesis
↓
Assessment
↓
Decision
↓
Outcome
↓
Learning
```

while also preserving:

```text
Assumptions
Alternatives
Confidence
Uncertainty
Known Gaps
Bias Reviews
Knowledge Patterns
```

The goal is to make analytical reasoning:

* Transparent
* Auditable
* Reusable
* Machine-assisted
* Continuously improvable

***

# Analyst Workflow

## Step 1: Create Investigation

```text
Investigation
 ├─ Title
 ├─ Purpose
 ├─ Priority
 ├─ Domain
 └─ Lead Analyst
```

Rule:

```text
Investigations contain facts,
not conclusions.
```

***

## Step 2: Record Observations

Observation fields:

```text
Who observed it?
When?
Source?
Confidence?
Exact observation?
```

Good:

```text
Domain xyz.com registered
2026-07-10
```

Bad:

```text
APT attack underway
```

The latter is a hypothesis.

***

## Step 3: Create Hypotheses

Minimum recommended:

```text
3 hypotheses
```

Example:

```text
H1
State actor

H2
Criminal actor

H3
Benign research project
```

Purpose:

Prevent anchoring bias.

***

## Step 4: Map Evidence

Every observation should be linked.

```text
Observation
→ Supports Hypothesis

Observation
→ Contradicts Hypothesis

Observation
→ Neutral
```

Do not permit unlinked evidence.

***

## Step 5: Record Assumptions

Every hypothesis should identify:

```text
Assumption
Criticality
Validation Status
```

Example:

```text
Infrastructure ownership unchanged

Criticality: High
```

***

## Step 6: Record Indicators

Indicators are predictions.

Example:

```text
If H1 true:

Additional domains should appear
within 30 days.
```

Indicators test hypotheses.

***

## Step 7: Produce Assessment

Assessment contains:

```text
Likelihood
Confidence
Rationale
```

Example:

```text
Likely
Moderate Confidence
```

***

# Confidence Documentation Standard

## Likelihood

Represents probability.

```text
REMOTE
POSSIBLE
ROUGHLY EVEN
LIKELY
HIGHLY LIKELY
```

Suggested ranges:

```text
REMOTE          0%-20%
POSSIBLE        20%-45%
ROUGHLY EVEN    45%-55%
LIKELY          55%-80%
HIGHLY LIKELY   80%-100%
```

***

## Confidence

Represents evidentiary quality.

```text
LOW
MODERATE
HIGH
```

Low:

```text
Little evidence
Conflicting evidence
Sparse collection
```

Moderate:

```text
Some corroboration
```

High:

```text
Multiple corroborated sources
```

# Reasoning Ontology

The machine should understand:

```text
Observation
    ≠
Hypothesis

Hypothesis
    ≠
Fact

Assumption
    ≠
Evidence

Confidence
    ≠
Likelihood

Indicator
    =
Predicted observation
```

These distinctions are fundamental.

# Golden Rule of the Platform

The most important documentation statement should be:

```text
Store observations as facts.

Store hypotheses as explanations.

Store assumptions separately.

Store confidence separately from likelihood.

Never overwrite historical reasoning.

Record what was believed,
why it was believed,
how confident analysts were,
what decisions were made,
and what eventually happened.
```

That principle makes the platform valuable not just as a database, but as a long-term reasoning, learning, and knowledge-generation system for both humans and machine-learning models.
